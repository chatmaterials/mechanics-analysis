# Mechanics Notes

- Positive-definite elastic tensors are a useful compact stability heuristic, but they do not replace full symmetry-specific Born criteria.
- Pugh-ratio and Poisson-ratio descriptors are screening hints, not definitive statements about fracture behavior.
- Candidate ranking based on bulk modulus and residual stress is useful for triage, but it is not a substitute for a full mechanical-property campaign.
- Hardness and anisotropy estimates are useful compact descriptors, but they remain model-dependent approximations rather than direct measured properties.
- EOS fit residuals are useful for screening noisy energy-volume datasets, but they do not replace a proper physically motivated EOS model.
